## Design Context

### Users
College and university students using a desktop Electron app during their own study sessions. They open the app right before sitting down to study — they're already in focus mode, not browsing. Primary use case: starting a timed study session, having focus tracked automatically, reviewing results afterward. Secondary: checking history over time. Sessions are typically 25–120 minutes. The app runs alongside their actual work (notes, browser, textbooks).

### Brand Personality
**Calm, precise, honest.** The app is a serious study partner, not a productivity gamification toy. It never cheerleads. It never lies about your focus score. It doesn't interrupt unless you're already distracted. Brand words: *measured, unobtrusive, credible*.

Anti-references: Notion (too playful), Forest (gamified, infantilizing), Todoist (productivity theater). The app should feel more like a research instrument than a consumer app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme — students study at night, in dark rooms, with screens dimmed. The dark background is functional, not aesthetic posturing. Color is used sparingly as signal: green = earned focus (real study time), amber = distraction, orange = primary action only. Everything else is neutral. Typography is the primary design tool — sizes and weights create hierarchy without decorative color.

### Design Principles
1. **Never interrupt unless already interrupted.** The UI should recede during study. Only the timer and status should draw attention on the TimerScreen.
2. **Green means earned.** Reserve #22c55e strictly for: focused status, good scores (70%+), correct check-in answers, Pro subscription badge. Nowhere else.
3. **Three decision points maximum on StartSessionScreen.** Subject, topic, start. Everything else is secondary or hidden.
4. **Explain the numbers.** The focus score is the core metric — it must never be a black box. Every metric shown should have a path to understanding what feeds it.
5. **Paywall should invite, not punish.** Show what the user is missing; don't aggressively obscure it.
